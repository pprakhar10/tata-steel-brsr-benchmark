FY2022-23:
FY2022-23 is weak as a benchmark year for the related-party metrics because the four RPT percentage metrics are missing across Tata, JSW, SAIL, and Jindal, so all are ranked last under the rule. The more meaningful FY2023 comparison is therefore on sustainability capex and R&D orientation. Tata leads capex at 23% on a consolidated basis and ties with JSW at 100% on R&D alignment, while SAIL reports 0% capex and 9.02% R&D, and Jindal reports only 0.02% on both sustainability capex and R&D. Tata’s FY2023 boundary is materially different because it is consolidated and includes Indian plus international operations, so its leadership cannot be read as pure standalone India superiority. Still, from a management perspective, Tata already had a broader sustainability investment frame than SAIL and Jindal. Jindal’s low FY2023 base also reflects the early stage of its later FY2024/FY2025 sustainability investment ramp.

FY2023-24:
FY2023-24 is the first meaningful year for RPT benchmarking. SAIL is the governance concentration leader on investments, loans/advances, purchases, and sales, with 81.66% related-party investments, 1.15% loans/advances, 6.66% purchases, and just 0.06% sales. Tata is mixed: strong on sales at 12% but weak on purchases at 40% and high on related-party investments at 96%. Jindal sits between SAIL and Tata/JSW on purchases at 7% and investments at 89%, but is high on loans/advances at 100%. JSW shows high related-party concentration in investments, loans/advances, and sales. On sustainability capex, Jindal leads at 23.42%, ahead of Tata at 18%, while Tata and JSW tie at 100% R&D. The structural story is clear: SAIL’s standalone PSU operating structure reduces RPT intensity, while Tata/JSW/Jindal group ecosystems create more related-party flows that require stronger governance controls rather than simple ratio-based judgement.

FY2024-25:
FY2024-25 shows Tata’s strongest overall position in the combined theme, but not across all RPT metrics. Tata leads sales-to-related-parties at 13.04%, leads sustainability capex at 37%, and ties for rank 1 on R&D at 100%. However, Tata is still behind SAIL and Jindal on related-party investments, behind SAIL on loans/advances, and last on related-party purchases at 37.78%. SAIL remains the cleanest RPT concentration benchmark on investments, loans/advances, and purchases, reflecting its standalone public-sector structure and internalised plant/mine/unit footprint. Jindal is the strongest stainless peer on low purchase concentration and improving sustainability R&D, rising to 51.98%, supported by product-longevity, corrosion-resistance, import-substitution, and renewable/WHRB/ZLD-linked projects. JSW remains numerically high on RPT concentration but ties Tata on R&D. For Tata senior management, the headline is: FY2025 sustainability investment leadership is strong, sales-side RPT exposure is best-in-peer, but purchase-side and investment-side related-party concentration remain the key governance watchpoints.
