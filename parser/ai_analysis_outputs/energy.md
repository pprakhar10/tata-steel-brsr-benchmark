It reported the lowest total energy at 30.28 million GJ and the best physical energy intensity at 19.15 GJ/tcs, reflecting a smaller stainless-steel boundary, EAF/recycling advantages and a less energy-heavy footprint than integrated BF-BOF peers. JSW was stronger than Tata and SAIL on absolute total energy and physical intensity, while Tata had the lowest energy per rupee of turnover in the standardised table. Tata’s FY2023 comparison must be treated carefully because the table flags Tata as consolidated, including Indian and international operations, whereas other company-years are generally standalone. SAIL’s FY2023 physical intensity was missing and therefore ranked last by rule. On renewable energy, JSW was already ahead at 1.43 million GJ versus Tata’s 220,000 GJ, SAIL’s 127,200 GJ and Jindal’s 121,060 GJ.

FY2023-24:
Jindal Stainless remained the energy leader on absolute total energy, non-renewable energy, non-renewable electricity, non-renewable fuel and physical intensity. Its total energy was 35.14 million GJ and physical intensity was 19.97 GJ/tcs, still materially below Tata at 28.3 GJ/tcs, JSW at 23.79 GJ/tcs and SAIL at 32 GJ/tcs. JSW led renewable energy at 1.98 million GJ and renewable electricity at 1.85 million GJ, indicating stronger renewable scaling than Tata. Tata improved total and non-renewable energy versus FY2023 but ranked only 3rd on total energy and physical intensity. SAIL was the weakest on total energy and physical intensity, and its non-renewable electricity value is marked standardised/corrected, requiring caution. Jindal’s BRSR indicates PAT overachievement at Jajpur and Hisar, supporting its strong intensity position.

FY2024-25:
Jindal Stainless is the strongest overall performer in FY2025. It leads on total energy at 32.05 million GJ, non-renewable energy at 30.74 million GJ, non-renewable electricity at 3.06 million GJ, non-renewable fuel at 27.68 million GJ and physical intensity at 16.36 GJ/tcs. JSW leads on renewable energy and renewable electricity, reaching 3.23 million GJ and 3.06 million GJ respectively. SAIL improved materially in total energy and physical intensity, reaching 519.43 million GJ and 27 GJ/tcs, supported by disclosed CDQ, TRT, CDI, waste heat recovery, walking beam furnaces, renewable capacity plans and green hydrogen pilots. Tata’s FY2025 position is mixed but weak on the most important intensity metric: it ranks 4th on physical energy intensity at 28.3 GJ/tcs and 4th on total energy. Tata’s renewable programme is strategically relevant, but peers are currently converting renewable procurement and process-efficiency measures into better disclosed energy outcomes faster.
