Tata declined from 7% to 6% and stayed at 6%; Tata’s FY2024-25 BRSR explains that it serves international clients through subsidiaries but separately discloses direct India exports, with standalone export revenue of ₹7,700.45 crore against total revenue of ₹132,516.66 crore, or 6%. Strategically, Tata’s low-to-moderate export share should not be read as weak market capability; the BRSR notes that Tata serves international customers through subsidiaries, so direct standalone export percentage captures only part of Tata Steel’s international market exposure.

FY2022-23:
The overall FY2022-23 financial-profile leader is Tata Steel if balance-sheet strength is weighted alongside turnover and exports: Tata led net worth at ₹103,082.0 crore and was nearly tied with JSW on turnover after normalising raw INR-style entries into INR crore. JSW was the turnover leader at ₹130,039.0 crore, narrowly ahead of Tata’s ~₹129,006.6 crore, while Jindal Stainless was the clear export-intensity leader at 15.49%. The main structural difference is portfolio and boundary: Tata’s FY2022-23 data is flagged consolidated, including international operations, while JSW, SAIL, and Jindal are standalone. SAIL’s scale remained material at ~₹104,447.4 crore turnover but lower than Tata/JSW, and its annual report highlights a difficult global steel cycle with energy crisis, inflation, supply-chain disruption, rate hikes, and volatile steel prices. Jindal Stainless was the laggard on absolute turnover and net worth because it is a stainless-focused player, not a broad BF-BOF integrated carbon-steel major, although it led export contribution.

FY2023-24:
Tata Steel was the strongest overall FY2023-24 performer, ranking 1st on turnover and net worth, with ~₹142,913.3 crore turnover and ₹134,137.5 crore net worth. JSW remained the closest absolute-scale peer at ₹133,609.0 crore turnover and ₹67,903.0 crore net worth, but the gap to Tata was wider on capital base than on revenue. SAIL was stable on turnover at ~₹104,545.1 crore and improved net worth to ₹54,130.5 crore, but remained behind Tata and JSW. Jindal Stainless continued to be the export leader at 17.07% and improved turnover to ~₹38,356.0 crore, demonstrating strong stainless demand and international market reach, but it remained the smallest by absolute financial scale. Tata’s FY2023-24 BRSR also explains a methodology issue: prior-year figures were restated following mergers and revised reporting boundaries, so the apparent improvement should be read with the restated standalone basis in mind.

FY2024-25:
FY2024-25 is mixed but still favourable for Tata on operating scale. Tata ranked 1st on turnover at ₹132,516.7 crore, ahead of JSW at ₹125,678.0 crore and SAIL at ₹101,716.0 crore, while Jindal Stainless stayed much smaller at ~₹40,816.9 crore. Tata’s BRSR confirms standalone turnover of ₹132,516.66 crore, with steel products contributing 93%, and notes that consolidated turnover was much larger at ₹218,542.51 crore due to India, Europe, Thailand, and other entities. On net worth, JSW is the reported leader because Tata’s FY2024-25 value is missing in the standardised table; this should be treated as a data gap, not proof of weaker financial position. On exports, Jindal Stainless remained the clear leader at 11.69%, supported by stainless-steel global applications and 49 international markets, while Tata improved to 2nd because it held exports stable at 6% and JSW fell to 5.5%.
